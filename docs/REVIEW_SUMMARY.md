# Review Summary

This hand-off is based on `manuscript/novel.md` and the available pass notes in `docs/passes/`. Only one pass note file is present: `docs/passes/pass-1-notes.md`. So "across the passes" currently means the controversies and open questions preserved by that pass, rather than competing notes from multiple reviewers.

## Executive Read

The novel now works as a complete rough draft. Its strongest achievement is the conversion of a friendship trip into a horror system where ordinary sound, practical competence, male care, grief, and procedure all become dangerous. The first half establishes the men through gear, jokes, irritation, and embodied water travel; the back half turns those same materials into rules, traps, and damage.

The draft is also long, heavily procedural, and sometimes too articulate about its own themes. The next human pass should be a shape-and-compression pass, not another expansion pass. Preserve the concrete work and the sound logic; cut places where the prose explains the governing idea after the scene has already demonstrated it.

## What Was Controversial

Length is the largest judgment call. The pass notes say the draft grew to about 138,500 words to solve underdevelopment. That expansion did its job, but the manuscript now spends very long runs on wind, loading, camp rebuilding, route debate, boat trim, and gear accounting. Much of this is good and necessary, especially the failed departure and remaking of Second Camp at `novel.md:5127`, `novel.md:5153`, and `novel.md:5321`, and the bad crossing sequence at `novel.md:6453` through `novel.md:6819`. Still, the human revision should decide how much procedural duration the book truly wants.

The entity remains deliberately unexplained. This is probably right. The best moments make it feel like a listening system, not a monster with a clean mythology: the first list of copied sounds at `novel.md:6050`, the gear-language realization at `novel.md:6150`, the empty repetition of Will's invitation at `novel.md:6204`, and the later proof that the planted phrase is useful precisely because it is emotionally flat at `novel.md:13959` and `novel.md:14034`. The risk is that some late placements can read close to tactical intelligence rather than limited association, especially the way voices appear at critical crossings and decision points.

The Mike-centered ending is a strong but cold choice. The last intrusion is not Alex's grief voice or Will's intimacy, but Mike's authority voice: the throat-clear at `novel.md:15463`, followed by "We're exactly where we ought to be" at `novel.md:15475`, and the final refusal of comfort at `novel.md:15493`. This aligns the ending with command, route certainty, and Joel's lifelong susceptibility to authority. It may frustrate readers who expect the final wound to be the more intimate Joel-and-Will friendship wound or the more mournful Alex wound. I think the Mike choice is structurally right, but it should be protected by making Mike's authority arc unmistakable earlier.

Will's death is the most delicate intellectual choice. It is good that he is not stupid. He correctly identifies that the entity uses old sound without understanding why the sound hurts: see `novel.md:12857`, `novel.md:12951`, `novel.md:13095`, and `novel.md:13155`. His fatal mistake is believing the limit can be used safely. The death itself honors that tension at `novel.md:13294`, `novel.md:13410`, `novel.md:13530`, and `novel.md:13590`. Revision should preserve the balance: Will is right about the mechanism and wrong about what a useful truth can do for a living body.

The unnamed fiancee is a real tradeoff. Her namelessness preserves Joel's damaged relationship to names and voice, but it keeps her slightly functional. She enters mostly as "his fiancee's voice" at `novel.md:14742` and `novel.md:15006`, as a body he can verify at `novel.md:15081`, and as a careful partner in the domestic aftermath at `novel.md:15207` and `novel.md:15287`. Those scenes are good, but she may need one or two more specific, non-therapeutic details so she feels like a person with her own life, not only a witness to Joel's damage.

The notebook is powerful and maybe too clean. It begins plausibly as an ugly list or "ghost spreadsheet" at `novel.md:6062`, becomes rules and shared law by `novel.md:6422`, hardens into survival doctrine at `novel.md:13747`, and then returns as evidence and trauma object at `novel.md:15303` through `novel.md:15333`. Keep it, but watch for places where the notebook names the theme a beat after action has already made the point.

## Still Unfinished

The manuscript needs compression and chapter shaping. The longest middle chapters are not bad, but several cover similar movements: establish a physical problem, hear a copied sound, write or refine a rule, debate whether the rule is enough, perform a grim practical task. This is the book's engine, but the revision should vary the torque. Good candidates for careful trimming include the windbound material around `novel.md:5125`, the second-night rule material around `novel.md:5734`, the bad crossing around `novel.md:6453`, and the cabin sequence around `novel.md:8661`.

Some thematic sentences are excellent individually but accumulate. Examples worth auditing include "the language that kept people alive could also be used to move them" at `novel.md:5403`, the gear-language explanation at `novel.md:6150`, the sight manifesto at `novel.md:7166`, and "writing removed tone" at `novel.md:12263`. These are often true and beautifully phrased. The question is whether the reader still needs them each time.

The official aftermath should get a credibility pass. The 911 call at `novel.md:14752`, the responder scene beginning at `novel.md:14832`, the map interview at `novel.md:14872`, and the official-story formation at `novel.md:15016` all feel emotionally right. A human with emergency response, search-and-rescue, law enforcement, or wilderness incident knowledge should check terminology, timing, and what questions would be asked when. The scene should stay restrained, but its procedural confidence matters because the whole book has trained the reader to care about procedure.

Route and gear continuity still need a cold audit. The draft has many concrete objects that must remain where the story says they are: Highlander key, notebook, bear spray, food barrel, blue bag, canoe, paddles, cracked thwart, stove, hatchet, map case, tarp, phones. Examples of object trails that must stay airtight: the Highlander key staged in Mike's PFD at `novel.md:697`, tracked at the cabin at `novel.md:8855`, and transferred to Joel's survival logic at `novel.md:14425`; the blue bag introduced into the late tactic at `novel.md:13929`, verified at `novel.md:14034`, and echoed at `novel.md:14714`.

The bear-plausibility thread should be calibrated. Alex's death needs to be plausibly classifiable by the survivors and officials without fully satisfying that explanation. The scene does this well when Mike needs the word "bear" at `novel.md:4001`, when the evidence partly fits at `novel.md:4007`, and when it fails to fit at `novel.md:4011`. Preserve that ambiguity. Do not let the scene become either obviously supernatural to the characters too early or cleanly animal-plausible enough to weaken the entity.

## What Feels Good And Strong

The opening voice is strong. The trip begins in ordinary logistics, irritation, and affection: gear and canoes at `novel.md:10`, Joel's place in the group at `novel.md:122`, the Highlander setup at `novel.md:130`, and the first sound baseline at `novel.md:28`. This gives the horror a real vocabulary to corrupt.

Alex is much stronger than a comic relief casualty. The draft gives him performative annoyance and real usefulness before death. His small kindnesses and competence show in the landing/strap material around `novel.md:2803` and `novel.md:2837`, and his death lands because Joel has been actively annoyed by him in a believable way before grief makes that annoyance obscene at `novel.md:3961` and `novel.md:4057`.

Mike's arc is one of the book's strongest structures. He is useful before he is dangerous. His route confidence and checking habit begin as competence at `novel.md:609`, `novel.md:639`, and `novel.md:697`; the cabin sequence shows both the usefulness and fatal limit of that authority at `novel.md:8703`, `novel.md:8937`, and `novel.md:9025`; his final absence still controls the exit through the key and the Highlander at `novel.md:14429` and `novel.md:14662`.

Will and Joel become the emotional core. Their written arguments and old friendship history make the last third more than a survival exercise. The church-basement memory under Joel's hand over Will's mouth at `novel.md:13352`, the argument over understanding versus survival at `novel.md:13410` through `novel.md:13422`, and Joel backing away from Will at `novel.md:13590` are among the draft's best material.

The rules are thematically strong because they are behavior, not lore. "No sound without sight" begins in the second night at `novel.md:6112`, becomes the visible/living-person rule at `novel.md:6226` and `novel.md:6346`, hardens into written law at `novel.md:13747`, and is finally shown to be necessary but not sufficient at `novel.md:9255`. That last point is crucial: the book should never imply that Joel solved the entity. He survived one route through damage and luck.

The planted phrase works. "Blue bag under the front" is ugly, practical, and emotionally inert. Joel creates it as bait/noise with no grief attached at `novel.md:13959` and `novel.md:13985`; the entity repeats it at `novel.md:14011`, `novel.md:14204`, and `novel.md:14437`; and the phrase remains unsettling even after rescue at `novel.md:14714` and `novel.md:15121`. Keep its plainness.

The ending is strong. The final pages make home another acoustic environment rather than a safe epilogue. Joel's house becomes a route at `novel.md:15235`, his fiancee has to adapt to the visual-confirmation rule at `novel.md:15417`, and the final kitchen voice at `novel.md:15475` refuses both supernatural certainty and trauma-only dismissal. The last line, `novel.md:15493`, lands.

## What Still Feels Murky

The entity's limit is conceptually clear but should be audited for experiential clarity. It repeats, associates, recombines, and places sound. It does not understand meaning. That is stated or dramatized at `novel.md:6246`, `novel.md:12632`, `novel.md:13061`, and `novel.md:13700`. But when it uses the right voice at the right tactical moment, the reader may feel intelligence whether or not the prose denies it. The revision should check every copied sound against four questions: did it hear this, why this source, why this location, and is the use associative rather than strategic?

Sight as a rule is intentionally unstable. The draft smartly complicates it: sight saves Will briefly at `novel.md:13400`, but sight also fails Mike because visual confirmation has to be continuous at `novel.md:9255`, and even sight can be tricked by attention during Will's death at `novel.md:13574`. This is good murk, but the revision should make sure readers understand the difference between "sight is real" and "sight is sufficient." Joel should not seem to discover a universal rule.

Joel's withholding from officials and family is emotionally right but ethically and procedurally murky. He gives enough to find bodies but withholds the sound rules and notebook at `novel.md:14920`, edits his account at `novel.md:14958`, and allows a plausible official version to form at `novel.md:15054` and `novel.md:15127`. This is one of the book's most humanly uncomfortable choices. It should remain uncomfortable. The question for revision is whether the manuscript wants Joel's withholding to read as necessary, damaged, selfish, merciful, or all of those.

The home ambiguity is strong but fragile. The kitchen voice can be read as the entity following him, trauma breaking through, or the novel refusing to distinguish those categories. The passage earns that ambiguity with the rational alternatives at `novel.md:15451` through `novel.md:15471`. Do not over-explain it. But make sure the preceding domestic chapter gives enough human specificity that the ending damages a real home, not an abstract aftermath chamber.

## Character Development Still Needed

Joel needs a sharper cost line. He survives by becoming less answerable, less available, and more dependent on sight, lists, and refusal. The draft already shows this well in the ambulance withholding at `novel.md:14958`, the difficulty answering living family at `novel.md:15195`, the domestic lists at `novel.md:15237`, and the fiancee doorway rule at `novel.md:15417`. Revision should push the moral cost, not make him more heroic. He should feel lucky, damaged, and somewhat frightening by the end.

Will needs protection from any reading that he dies because the plot needs someone to answer. His theory is useful and partly correct. The best material for this is at `novel.md:12857`, `novel.md:12953`, `novel.md:13191`, and `novel.md:13554`. Keep his intelligence visible through the end. If cutting, do not cut so much that his fatal test becomes mere genre compulsion.

Mike needs the full dignity of competence. He should not become only arrogance. Lines like `novel.md:609`, `novel.md:8703`, `novel.md:9025`, and `novel.md:9131` show that he is often right, often useful, and killed by the same trait that has kept the group functioning. Preserve that.

Alex is working, but distribute his quieter kindnesses carefully before death. His stolen voice hurts most when the reader remembers a full person, not just jokes. Keep material like `novel.md:2837`, `novel.md:3961`, and Joel's report to Alex's brother at `novel.md:15201`.

The fiancee needs a little more personhood. The strongest current beats are her non-sentimental patience at `novel.md:15207`, her interpretation of the lists at `novel.md:15255`, her honest boundary at `novel.md:15287`, and her acceptance of the doorway rule at `novel.md:15417`. Add or sharpen one or two details about her work, habits, anger, family, or private cost. Do not turn her into a therapy function.

The families and responders are brief but mostly effective. Will's mother at `novel.md:15137`, Mike's sister at `novel.md:15161`, Alex's brother at `novel.md:15183`, Deputy Harris at `novel.md:14836`, Elise at `novel.md:14862`, and Reardon at `novel.md:14878` all make the outside world feel inhabited. The revision should keep them concrete and restrained.

## Recommended Human Revision Order

1. Do a chapter-level shape pass. For each chapter, identify the physical problem, emotional pressure, mimicry escalation, and changed condition at the end. Cut scenes or paragraphs that repeat a prior chapter's function without deepening it.

2. Run a mimicry ledger. Every copied phrase or sound should have a source, a reason it is available, and a limited associative use. Keep some ordinary natural sounds ordinary so the woods do not become only the entity.

3. Audit route, day, and gear continuity. Treat the Highlander key, notebook, blue bag, food barrel, bear spray, boats, paddles, map case, and phone service as evidence chains.

4. Protect Will's intellectual tragedy. The reader should feel both that Will is right and that Joel is right to refuse him.

5. Compress theme-naming. Keep the concrete action first. Where a scene already makes the idea clear, cut the sentence that explains it unless the phrasing is doing irreplaceable voice work.

6. Give the fiancee one or two more specific, independent human details while preserving Joel's POV and the power of her partial unknowability.

7. Have a knowledgeable reader check the emergency response, search, reporting, and official statement material.

8. Preserve the final ambiguity. The ending should not answer whether the entity followed Joel. It should leave the reader with the worse problem the draft already articulates: after this kind of survival, even ordinary sound can no longer prove its innocence.
