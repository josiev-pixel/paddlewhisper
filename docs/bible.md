# Story Bible

## Project

Working title: `Horror Canoe`.

Genre: literary wilderness horror / psychological survival horror.

Core sentence: four old friends on a remote canoe route are hunted by something that can only use the sounds they give it.

## POV

Close third through Joel by default. Stay inside his observations, doubts, fatigue, and practical reasoning. Do not explain the entity from outside his knowledge.

## Style

- Plain, concrete, physical prose.
- Occasional lyrical pressure when earned by water, weather, dread, grief, exhaustion, or uncanny sound.
- Dialogue is rough, funny, interruptive, specific, and shaped by old friendship.
- Avoid ornate horror language, easy jump scares, and over-explaining emotion.

## Setting

Fictional remote northern canoe route near Canada. Terrain includes black spruce, jack pine, granite shelves, tannin water, alder-choked channels, beaver dams, portages, marsh, gravel bars, old maintenance traces, and sparse camps.

Weather is early September: warm exertion, cold mornings, mosquito dusk, sudden wind over lakes, rain, fog, and hard nighttime quiet.

## Route

- Day 0: Drive, motel near access road.
- Day 1: Stage Highlander at North Road Bridge; launch from South Access / Old Landing; first lake and portage; First Camp on granite shelf.
- Day 2: Beaver work, wider water, rough portage, Second Camp. Alex dies at night.
- Day 3: Shock, bear theory, body, windbound delay at Second Camp.
- Day 4: Bad crossing, forced landing, emergency camp.
- Day 5: Old map, maintenance trace, abandoned cabin, Mike dies.
- Day 6: Joel and Will make written rules, return to the water, hear Joel's voice, repair compromised gear.
- Day 7: Final push, Will dies, Joel plants a phrase and crosses toward the take-out.
- Day 8: Joel reaches North Road Bridge and later goes home.

## Vehicles And Gear

- Will's truck carries canoes and main gear to South Access / Old Landing and remains there.
- Mike's old Toyota Highlander is staged at North Road Bridge / intended take-out.
- Will's truck key is sealed in Will's pack.
- Mike's Highlander key is carried by Mike early and must become a practical problem after his death.
- No satellite messenger or emergency beacon.
- Two tandem canoes. Launch pairings: Mike back / Will front; Joel back / Alex front.
- The group uses plain canoe words in dialogue: front, back, left, right, boat, canoe.
- They say portage for the trail; carry means physically carrying something.
- Default footwear is hiking sandals.

## Characters

### Joel

Software developer, originally St. Louis, now Madison. Engaged. Quiet, rational, dry, observant. Childhood church friend of Will and no longer religious. Competent but not the primary planner. He survives through restraint, pattern recognition, and silence.

Signature seed: "Wait. Just wait."

### Will

Recently unemployed quality-control engineer for an LED company. Talkative, anxious, technical, hobby-heavy, inappropriate, useful, and exhausting. Childhood friend of Joel; middle-school/high-school friend of Mike. Fishes whenever possible. His need to explain and correct becomes fatal.

Signature seeds: "That's not how that works," "No, no, no," "I'm just saying," "There's a difference," "You guys hear that?", dry cough/laugh.

### Mike

Canoe reseller, financially precarious, brash, negative, competent, route-certain, and controlling. Met Will in middle school. Uses reassurance as control. Dies trying to recover authority or fix a route failure.

Signature seeds: "We're fine," "We're exactly where we ought to be," "It opens up after this," "Map says there's a cabin," "Just keep moving," throat clear before decisions.

### Alex

Pacific Northwest supply-chain worker in Seattle; grew up in Alaska. Loud, performative, funny, abrasive, picky about food, less canoe-skilled but physically game. Came into the group later. Dies first and must remain vivid in absence.

Signature seeds: "Quit mothering me," "You coming or writing a poem?", "Jesus, listen to this guy," "I'm taking a piss. Nobody panic," "Hold up," hard bark of laughter.

## Entity Rules

- It can mimic sounds, voices, phrases, laughter, coughs, gear, and environmental noises it has heard.
- It cannot read minds.
- It cannot invent freely from nothing.
- It is strongest when it uses ordinary group habits.
- It is better at tone than meaning.
- It may use phrases in the wrong context, exposing limitation.
- It is never fully seen or explained.
- Its threat is increased by distance, weather, fatigue, grief, darkness, and poor visual confirmation.

## Death Logic

Alex dies because he responds to the edge of camp and wants the moment to become a story.

Mike dies because he needs to restore control after the old map and cabin fail.

Will dies because he cannot stop answering, testing, and correcting.

Joel survives because he can stop answering.

## Sound Bank

Active seeds from chapters 1-5:

- Alex: "Quit mothering me," hard laugh, "You coming or writing a poem?", "Hold up."
- Mike: "We're fine," "We're exactly where we ought to be," "It opens up after this," "Map says there's a cabin," "Just keep moving," throat clear.
- Will: "That's not how that works," "I'm just saying," "There's a difference," "No, no, no," "You guys hear that?", cough/laugh.
- Joel: "Wait. Just wait."
- Gear/camp: food barrel latch, strap buckle, paddles knocking, tent zipper, stove click, hatchet tap, mug on granite, tarp line, hull scrape, painter rope tapping, boot scrape.
- Woods/water: loon call, raven call, branch crack, alder movement, paddle drip, beaver work, fish jump.

## Ending Call

Working ending: Joel reaches North Road Bridge, survives official questioning, goes home, and hears Mike's throat clear followed by "We're exactly where we ought to be." The line is thematically central and ambiguous without requiring the entity to be explained.
