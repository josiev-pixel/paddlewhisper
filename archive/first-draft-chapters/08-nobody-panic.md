# Chapter 8 - Nobody Panic

For a second the camp held its shape.

Mike by the fire. Will kneeling beside the food barrel. Joel half turned toward the brush. Alex's headlamp a small white place beyond the alder.

Then Alex said, "Hold up."

Not loud. Not frightened. The way he had said it all day when rope caught or mud held his foot.

Will stood. "Alex?"

The headlamp jerked once, dipped, and came back up sideways.

"What the hell," Alex said.

Brush moved. A heavy sound followed, not a roar, not anything clean enough to name. It was impact and wet alder and Alex's breath cut short. Mike grabbed the bear spray from beside his pack and ran toward the light. Joel was behind him before he knew he had moved. Will shouted Alex's name and kept shouting it, which made every direction seem like a direction.

The headlamp went out.

"Stop yelling," Mike snapped.

Will did not stop.

Joel's own lamp found alder stems, leaves, mud, the pale side of a broken branch. Something big moved just outside the beam and was gone before the light could decide on it. He saw a color that might have been hide, or wet bark, or his own fear making a body out of dark.

Mike sprayed into brush.

The orange cloud hit leaves and came back at them. Joel choked. His eyes burned. Will doubled over coughing. Mike swore and kept moving anyway.

They found Alex ten yards beyond the first alder screen.

At first Joel's mind refused scale. It gave him pieces. One sandal. One hand curled in mud. The headlamp strap. Blood black under white light. A torn place where the body should have remained ordinary.

"No, no, no," Will said, and now the words had weight.

Mike pushed him back with one arm. "Don't touch him."

"We have to--"

"Don't touch him."

Joel stood with his paddle in his hand because he had somehow brought it instead of anything useful. His lungs hurt from spray. The woods around them were quiet in the exact way quiet becomes a thing with edges.

"Bear," Mike said.

No one answered.

"It was a bear."

Alex's face was turned partly into the mud. Joel could not make himself look long and could not stop looking. Alex had been laughing twenty minutes earlier. He had been complaining about sauce. He had been impossible to ignore, and now the world had made him into evidence.

Will said, "Where is it?"

"Gone."

"How do you know?"

Mike turned on him. "Because we're standing here."

That was not an answer. It was only enough shape to stand inside for one more second.

They backed toward camp with their lights pointed out. Mike kept the bear spray in front of him though the canister was light now. Joel took Will by the pack strap once when Will drifted toward the body again.

"We can't leave him," Will said.

"We're not leaving him," Joel said.

But they did. They left him for the night because darkness made morality smaller than fear. They pulled the canoes closer. They put every pot and paddle within reach. They sat under the tarp with lights off and listened.

After a long time, from the brush beyond Alex, something made his hard bark of laughter.

Once.

Will made a sound like he had been hit.

Mike whispered, "No."

Joel did not know whether Mike meant he had not heard it, or that the world was not allowed to have done it.
