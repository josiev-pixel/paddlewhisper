# Chapter 15 - The Trace

The trace disappeared three times.

Each time Mike found it again, which made it harder to argue with him and easier for him to trust himself. He read cut stumps under moss, old drainage, the faint straightness of trees along a grade. He was good at this. That was the problem. Competence kept opening the next door.

Will's bandaged hand throbbed. He kept flexing it and then remembering not to. Joel watched his own feet because the ground wanted ankles. Wet leaves hid holes. Roots crossed the path at bad angles. Packs shifted and knocked against branches with small human sounds.

At a break they ate bars without speaking. Mike checked the Highlander key in the zip pocket of his PFD. He had kept the PFD on after leaving the boats, absurd and practical at once.

Will saw the gesture and raised his eyebrows.

Mike said, "Take-out doesn't help if we lose the key."

It was a reasonable sentence. Spoken aloud, it became material.

Joel wrote: key in Mike PFD.

Will read it and nodded.

The woods made direction strange. A cough came from ahead while Will stood behind Joel drinking water. Mike's voice came from the left saying, "It opens up after this," though the living Mike was silent and visible twenty feet ahead. Alex laughed behind them, bright and wrong.

Will pressed both hands over his ears.

Mike turned. "Don't."

Will lowered them. "Don't what?"

"Don't break."

"That's your advice?"

"Yes."

"Useful."

Mike's mouth tightened, but he let it go. They kept walking.

Late in the afternoon the trees thinned. Joel saw a hard dark line through spruce trunks. Roof. For one minute the whole day changed. Even Will moved faster. The cabin existed. A human thing, square and deliberate, set back from a small clearing where grass grew waist high around rusted cans.

Mike stopped at the edge of the clearing and looked back at them with something like vindication.

Will was too tired to deny him.

The cabin had a sagging roof, one intact shutter, one window boarded from inside, and a door hanging slightly open. No smoke. No antenna. No visible road beyond it, though an opening in the trees suggested where one had once entered.

"Careful," Joel said.

Mike almost smiled. "Now we're careful?"

Nobody answered.

The door groaned when Mike pushed it. The sound moved through the clearing and came back from the trees too late.

Inside smelled of mouse droppings, damp wood, old ash, and something mineral under the rot. Light came through roof gaps in thin white pieces. There was a table, two broken chairs, a rusted stove pipe, an empty shelf, and names cut into the wall by people who had been gone for years.

Will laughed once. Not humor. Exhaustion.

"Okay," he said. "Cabin."

Mike stood in the middle of the room with the map in his hand.

Outside, from the far side of the clearing, Mike's voice said, "Map says there's a cabin."
