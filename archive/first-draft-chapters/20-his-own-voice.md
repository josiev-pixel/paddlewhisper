# Chapter 20 - His Own Voice

After Joel heard himself, the route lost its last outside.

Before that, some part of him had believed the thing belonged to Alex's death, to Mike's mistake, to Will's noise, to the brush around Second Camp. His own voice proved otherwise. It had been near from the beginning, or near enough. At the landing. At the first water. At the places they had felt almost safe because nothing had yet happened.

Will kept looking at him as if Joel might explain what selfhood sounded like when taken.

Joel had no explanation. He had only rules.

They found the canoe cache late. The repaired boat was still under spruce boughs, but the boughs had been moved. Not scattered. Moved aside and laid in a neat fan on the mud. The better canoe was turned six feet from where they had left it. The painter rope had been looped twice around an alder stem and tied in a knot neither Joel nor Will recognized.

Will crouched beside it.

Joel touched his shoulder before he could speak.

Will took out the notebook.

Can it tie knots?

Joel looked at the rope. The knot was ugly but effective. It could have been made by a human hand, an animal's accident, or something imitating the result of hands without understanding comfort.

He wrote:

DOESN'T MATTER. CUT IT.

They cut the rope rather than untie it. Small decisions became offerings to speed.

The food barrel was still sealed. One dry bag had been dragged into mud and soaked through. The map case had grit in the seal. Mike's repair on the cracked thwart held when Joel pressed it, but barely.

Two men with two canoes made little sense. One usable canoe was faster but riskier. Two boats meant more gear and more mistakes. They chose one: the better hull, loaded low, with the repaired paddle as spare, the key in Joel's inner pocket, and most nonessential weight left under the spruce.

Will held up his fishing rods.

Joel shook his head.

Will's face changed more than Joel expected. The rods were not just rods. They were habit, hope, a version of Will that existed before voices made habit dangerous.

Will laid them under the canoe they were abandoning and covered them with boughs.

From the water came a hollow knock.

The canoe they had loaded sat still at their feet.

Another knock came from the fog beyond the first bend.

Will wrote:

It wants us to think boat/person.

Joel wrote:

yes.

Will wrote:

Then it has a goal.

Joel wrote:

So do we.

They launched without speaking. The channel ahead was narrow and dark, and the fog took the far bank in pieces.
