# Chapter 10 - Windbound

They did not leave.

By the time the boats were loaded, the lake beyond the channel had gone white at the tips. Wind came over it in steady pushes, laying the near grass flat and lifting spray off the farther chop. Mike stood at the landing with the map in both hands while Will stared past him.

"We can hug the left shore," Mike said.

Joel looked at the water. "Not with one boat half empty and everyone cooked."

"We are not staying here."

"I didn't say we stay."

"Then what are you saying?"

"I'm saying that crossing that because we're scared is still crossing that."

Will made a dry sound that might have been agreement or the beginning of collapse. "Backtrack?"

Mike shook his head. "Worse. More portages. Back through the beaver work. Past him."

Nobody used Alex's name.

They tried to wait the wind down. It rose instead. Low cloud came in from the west, and by noon rain had started, first as a mist and then as a cold insistence that soaked collars and hands. They dragged the canoes higher, tied them separately, and put the tarp back up over the same miserable center of camp.

The body remained beyond the alder.

That was how Joel thought of it now. Not Alex. The body. The covered place. The problem they could not solve without turning their friend into logistics.

Will paced under the tarp until Mike told him to sit or go be useful elsewhere.

"Useful how?" Will said. "Do you have a department? Should I file for bear confusion?"

"Stop."

"No, because if it was a bear, then fine, it was a bear. Horrible, but fine. But bears do not talk."

"Nothing talked."

Will stared at him.

Mike said, "You heard what you heard after not sleeping."

"You heard it too."

"I heard a sound."

Joel watched rain gather on the tarp seam and fall in a steady leak beside the stove. He wanted Mike's answer to be true because it was narrow enough to live in. He also remembered the exact rhythm of Alex's line. The little irritation in it. The way it had belonged to the driveway before it belonged to blood.

Will coughed.

Joel turned toward him.

Will was on Joel's right, hunched under his rain jacket.

The cough had come from the left side of camp, near the food barrel.

Joel looked there and saw nothing but rain ticking on blue plastic.

"What?" Will said.

Joel shook his head.

Mike said, "We're fine."

His voice was flat. It did not reassure anyone. It only closed the conversation.

Rain thickened. The wind kept them pinned to the place where Alex had died, and by evening the camp seemed to have learned them. Zipper. Buckle. Cough. Latch. Paddle knocking once from the shore though the paddles were under the tarp.

Joel lay awake that night and understood nothing except that the thing outside did not need to be seen to get closer.
